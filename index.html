Abstract

Vision language models (VLMs) have been applied to several specific domains and have shown strong problem-solving capabilities. However, there are still some more complicated tasks that involve multidisciplinary knowledge and several subtasks that may interact with each other and have not been studied. Astronomical imaging is a typical example. Due to the complexity of the astronomical imaging process, both world-class astronomical organizations, such as NASA, and expert enthusiasts devote a great deal of time and effort. This is because the processes in astronomical imaging have complex underlying correlations that significantly influence one another, making the quality diagnosis of astronomical images challenging. In this paper, we propose AstroVLM, a collaborative multi-agent system for diagnosing the quality of astronomical images. Experiment results show that AstroVLM outperforms all baselines on astronomical imaging quality diagnosis tasks, achieving excellent performance in handling such complex applications.

The Challenge of Astronomical Imaging

Astronomical imaging is a complex, multi-stage process encompassing Preparation, Shooting, and Post-processing. Each stage contains numerous steps, from equipment matching and polar alignment to image stacking and noise reduction. An error at any point can cascade and affect the final quality, but may only become apparent much later. This intricate, long-chain workflow makes diagnosing the root cause of an issue a significant challenge, which AstroVLM aims to address.

Relevant Wordlists Construction

The process of constructing relevant wordlists for each agent. KeyBert extracts keywords from documents, and a synthesizer agent designs the wordlists based on each agent's role and the given keywords. This ensures each agent has access to a tailored knowledge base, improving relevance and accuracy.

Knowledge Graph Partitioning and Aggregation

An overview of ASK-RAG's partitioning and aggregation method. The process starts from the root knowledge graph and uses flow-based partitioning with relevant wordlists. Subsequently, similarity-based aggregation uses a GNN to combine graph structure and semantic information, creating refined sub-graphs for each agent.

Reasoning with Backtracking

A comparison of reasoning methods. Typical VLMs perform a blind search, which can lead to irrelevant and redundant results. In contrast, AstroVLM's Reasoning with Backtracking (RwB) process enables an accurate and reasonable diagnosis by structuring the search space into a Collaborative Reasoning Tree, greatly improving error localization.
